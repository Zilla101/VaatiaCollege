Health, Safety and Welfare

At Vaatia College, the physical and mental well-being of our students is our highest priority. We maintain a robust infrastructure designed to ensure a safe learning environment.

  • Medical Services: A fully equipped sickbay managed by professional medical staff available 24/7.
  • Security Partnership: Partnership with the Civil Defense Corps for 24/7 armed security across the campus.
  • Surveillance: Full CCTV coverage in classrooms, hostels, and throughout the college premises.
  • Controlled Access: Restricted access to the college from outsiders to maintain a secure environment.
  • Nutrition & Wellness: Balanced meals prepared under strict hygienic conditions to support physical growth.
Note: All students undergo mandatory health screenings to ensure specialized care where needed.

Learning & Cultural Vitality

Education at Vaatia goes beyond the classroom, fostering collaboration and celebrating the rich diversity of our students.

01

Peer-To-Peer Learning: Students are actively encouraged to collaborate, sharing knowledge and tutoring one another to build collective academic strength.

02

Social & Cultural Week: An annual celebration where students exhibit their unique ethnic backgrounds through traditional attire and interactive sessions.

03

Indigenous Traditions: Features local cooking competitions, indigenous meal exhibitions, and traditional games like Ayo.

Student Voice & Leadership

At Vaatia College, we believe in empowering students to lead and contribute to the growth of our community.

The Student Parliament

Our Student Parliament serves as a vital legislative organ, ensuring that student voices are heard and considered in the decision-making process. It provides a platform for civic engagement and democratic values.

Induction and Pastoral Care

Transitioning into secondary school can be a significant step. Our pastoral care system ensures no student walks alone during their journey.

01

Comprehensive Induction: A multi-day orientation program for new students to familiarize them with campus life.

02

The House System: Small, supportive communities where older students mentor younger ones.

03

Personal Tutors: Every student is assigned a tutor who monitors their academic and social progress.

Vaatia College Makurdi

Vaatia College is a privately owned school established in 2004. A small class size of 30 students per class and a student/teacher ratio of 7:1 encourages an effective student-teacher relationship.
